Health and healthy lifestyle

A healthy lifestyle is a set of habits and actions that help strengthen and maintain immunity and physical capabilities, and eliminate factors that negatively affect well-being and the body as a whole. A strict concept of a healthy lifestyle has not been formulated, so people often perceive it in their own way. For some, this is sports seven times a week, while others believe that they have begun to lead a healthy lifestyle by giving up alcohol and cigarettes.
The concept became widespread in the 1970s, when people realized that progress inevitably leads to a decrease in physical activity, nutritional costs and bad habits. Doctors began to note genetic predispositions to health problems and the ability to avoid complications by following certain daily rules.

A healthy lifestyle contributes to:
  • reducing the number of relapses of chronic diseases;
  • increasing endurance: a person is full of strength, less likely to experience physical and emotional fatigue;
  • improved appearance: healthy skin, hair and nails;
  • normalization of weight, which has a positive effect on the figure and quality of the body, physical capabilities;
  • reducing the incidence of ARVI diseases; illnesses progress faster and easier.

Healthy eating and diet

  1. Eating a wide variety of healthy foods helps to keep you in good health and protects you against chronic disease.
  2. Eating a well-balanced diet means eating a variety of foods from each of the 5 food groups daily, in the recommended amounts.
  3. It is also important to choose a variety of foods from within each food group.
  4. Takeaway foods, cakes, biscuits and soft drinks are examples of foods usually high in saturated fat, added salt or added sugars. They should be considered as extras to your usual diet and only eaten occasionally and in small amounts.
  5. The recommended number of serves for each food group is different for children, teenagers, women and men.
